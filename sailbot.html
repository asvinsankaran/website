Captain – UBC Sailbot

UBC Sailbot is a student engineering design team that builds fully autonomous sailboats. As team captain, I have led the development and integration of POLARIS, a 3-meter autonomous sailboat designed to collect oceanic and atmospheric data in the Pacific Ocean. I’ve worked with a team of ~70 students across software, electrical, and mechanical disciplines to design and build everything in-house — from custom autopilot algorithms to a self-righting carbon fiber hull.

My responsibilities include high-level project management, cross-subsystem integration, sponsorship, and leading reviews with industry mentors. I also actively mentor younger members and support hands-on mechanical and electrical development.

Technical Highlights

  • Designed and tested custom actuator boards for sail and rudder control
  • Led manufacturing and assembly of composite hull and keel
  • Developed a remote dashboard for real-time telemetry via Starlink
  • Integrated ROS2-based control systems with GPS, IMU, wind sensors
  • Organized and presented design reviews to industry and faculty
