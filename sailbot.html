I am currently the Captain of UBC Sailbot, an engineering design team of ~70 undergraduate students focussing on the design, construction, and testing of fully autonomous sailboats. Our previous project (pictured) is an 18ft sailboat we created from scratch over many years. Currently, we are in the design phase of a new project, capable of using this robotics technology to collect oceanic and atmospheric data.

Captain

I began this role in my second year, and have had the unique opportunity to make many improvements to our team’s structure, organization, and project management in the past 2.5 years.

  • Revising our team’s leadership organization and building formality and responsibility into the team structure
  • Spearheading a project management initiative by holding teams accountable to macro and micro-scale timelines
  • Scoping and defining the team and project’s 3 year plan
  • Teaching students about project management, leadership, and team-work
  • But also, teaching students about mechanical and electrical engineering
  • Managing large project budgets and dictating financial decisions, funding and grants
  • Creating presentation opportunities for students to develop their communication skills

Mechanical Lead

I was promoted to this role soon after my first year given the aptitude I had for the mechanical engineering on this project. In my time as a mechanical lead, I led electrical integration projects, as well as initial rudder design.

Some of the projects I led include:

  • Statistical modelling of our boat’s theoretical solar panel generation
  • Analysis of our boat’s power budgeting comparing consumption to generation
  • Drive electrical design decisions based on power consumption
  • Initial design of our boat’s rudder blade
  • Fluid simulation and calculation for rudder lift/drag
