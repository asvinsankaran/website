Projects & Roles

Custom 5 Axis 3D Printer - DISC Alloys

For the past year, I have been working at DISC Alloys on the development of a 5-Axis 3D printer for adaptation to the startup's industrial metal 3D printing technology. I began this project from scratch with lots of mechanical design in CAD before moving into manufacturing in the machine shop, assembly, and prototype testing. After 8 months of design and mechanical assembly, I completed the mechatronics fabrication and moved into firmware, software, and control algorithms using multivariable calculus.

Captain – UBC Sailbot

I am currently the Captain of UBC Sailbot, an engineering design team of ~70 undergraduate students focussing on the design, construction, and testing of fully autonomous sailboats.

For the past 3+ years, I've always spent more time working on Sailbot when I should have been studying for classes. This team provides a unique opportunity to lead some highly innovative challenges and very passionate students. And where I started off learning a lot about technical engineering, I now spend my time teaching younger students while focussing more on high-level project management and technical integration. 

Plasma Fusion Reactor Design - ENPH Capstone Project

In my senior year at UBC, I was working with the prestigious Berlinguette Lab at UBC to design and test a novel plasma fusion reactor.

JARVIS - Personal Project

I'm currently working on a DIY sky-cam-like robot suspended by 4 wires. Initially, I was working through the 2D control algorithm using computer vision on MATLAB (video). After which, I re-designed the hardware and am now improving the 3D controls algorithm, which has proven incredibly difficult.

Course Projects

CHONKY

In a small group, we designed, manufactured, and tested a fully autonomous robot to compete in the 2022 Engineering Physics Robot Summer Competition.

Computer Vision and Controls

With only a camera stream fed through a ROS node, we used computer vision to control a virtual car around the course and read licence plates using a CNN.

Car Steering Mechanical Design

Following Shigley's Mechanical Design textbook, I worked in a small group to redesign a car's steering actuation system without any electrical/motor aid.
